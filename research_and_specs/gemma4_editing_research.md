# Gemma 4 MEMIT same-entity locality research

## Bottom line

Gemma 4 is a plausible but unproven substrate for in-weight, MEMIT-class knowledge writing; the best near-term target is the dense Gemma 4 E4B variant because it fits a 24 GB GPU in BF16 with some headroom and exposes conventional gated-MLP `down_proj` modules in Hugging Face Transformers ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4), [Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py)). The evidence does **not** currently show that Gemma 4 solves same-entity multi-attribute locality; the strongest adjacent evidence instead predicts a model-general failure mode in subject-token MLP editing, where one subject representation entangles many attributes and edits to one relation can perturb other relations of the same subject ([ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229), [S2RKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06868), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774), [MEMIT-Merge](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.07322)). Published Gemma-specific editing evidence is sparse: AlphaEdit reports results on an unspecified “Gemma” model, but no primary source found here reports ROME/MEMIT/PMET/EMMET/WISE/GRACE/MEND same-entity locality on Gemma 4, and no primary source found here reports RippleEdits, S2RKE, FINE-KED, or MQuAKE on Gemma ([AlphaEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02355), [RippleEdits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.12976), [MQuAKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14795)). Source strength for that conclusion is **medium-high** for “no found primary evidence” and **medium** for the prediction, because it extrapolates from non-Gemma editing studies and Gemma architecture evidence rather than from a direct Gemma 4 same-entity experiment.

## Source-strength key

- **High** means an official Google/DeepMind document, official code repository, official Hugging Face configuration, or peer-reviewed/arXiv method paper directly supports the claim.
- **Medium** means the source is primary but indirect for the target question, such as a non-Gemma benchmark being used to infer likely behavior on Gemma.
- **Low** means the claim is a hypothesis, practical inference, or unverified implementation expectation that needs a primary experiment.
- SEO and marketing blogs were not used as evidence; the report cites official model cards/docs/code, Hugging Face configs, arXiv papers, and official repos only.

## 1. Exact architecture: Gemma 4 variants and Gemma 2/3 lineage

### Gemma 4 family-level facts

Gemma 4 is documented as a multimodal family with text output, with E2B/E4B/12B supporting text, image, and audio inputs, and 26B A4B/31B supporting text and image inputs ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4)). Gemma 4 uses a hybrid attention pattern that interleaves local sliding-window layers and full global layers, and the official model card says the final layer is always global while global layers use unified Keys/Values and p-RoPE ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4)). Gemma 4 uses a 262K-token vocabulary across the listed variants, and the official model card reports context lengths of 128K for E2B/E4B and 256K for 12B/26B A4B/31B ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4)). Source strength: **high**.

The Hugging Face Gemma 4 dense MLP implementation computes `down_proj(act(gate_proj(x)) * up_proj(x))`, so the exact dense text target for MEMIT-style edits is `model.layers.{i}.mlp.down_proj` in the Hugging Face module hierarchy ([Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py)). The official Google DeepMind JAX module names the output projection inside `FeedForward` as `linear`, so a JAX implementation would need to target the `linear` weight in the feed-forward module rather than the Hugging Face `down_proj` name ([google-deepmind/gemma `_modules.py`](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/google-deepmind/gemma/main/gemma/gm/nn/_modules.py)). Source strength: **high**.

Gemma 4 decoder blocks in Hugging Face apply RMSNorm around attention and feed-forward sublayers with `input_layernorm`, `post_attention_layernorm`, `pre_feedforward_layernorm`, and `post_feedforward_layernorm`; MoE blocks add a second set of feed-forward norms around the expert path ([Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py)). Gemma 4 configs set `final_logit_softcapping=30.0`, so causal-tracing analyses that use raw logits must account for the tanh soft-cap transformation in the model head ([Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py)). Source strength: **high**.

Per-layer embeddings in E2B/E4B are implemented in Hugging Face as `embed_tokens_per_layer` with a packed per-token, per-layer vector that is reshaped across decoder layers, combined with a projected context embedding, gated at each decoder layer, projected back to hidden size, normalized, and added residually ([Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py)). This PLE mechanism means an entity token can inject layer-specific information outside the standard residual stream path normally assumed by ROME/MEMIT causal tracing, but no primary source found here quantifies whether PLE helps or hurts same-entity locality ([Gemma 4 E2B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E2B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json)). Source strength: **high** for implementation, **low** for locality implication.

### Gemma 2 lineage

Gemma 2 is a decoder-only transformer with RoPE, approximate GeGLU feed-forward layers, alternating local sliding-window and global attention every other layer, attention logit soft-capping of 50.0, final logit soft-capping of 30.0, RMSNorm at the input and output of transformer sublayers, and grouped-query attention with two KV groups per query group in the released sizes ([Gemma 2 technical report](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/gemma/gemma-2-report.pdf)). Gemma 2’s official architecture table reports 2B/9B/27B model dimensions of 26/42/46 layers, hidden sizes of 2304/3584/4608, feed-forward dimensions of 18432/28672/73728, GQA heads of 8/16/32, KV heads of 4/8/16, and a 256128-token vocabulary with tied embeddings ([Gemma 2 technical report](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/gemma/gemma-2-report.pdf)). Source strength: **high**.

Gemma 2 uses a SentencePiece tokenizer with split digits, preserved whitespace, byte-level encodings, and a vocabulary of 256K entries, which differs from Gemma 4’s 262144 vocabulary reported in Gemma 4 configs and model card ([Gemma 2 technical report](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/gemma/gemma-2-report.pdf), [Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4)). Source strength: **high**.

### Gemma 3 lineage

Gemma 3 keeps the decoder-only transformer lineage, uses GQA with pre- and post-RMSNorm, replaces Gemma 2’s attention soft-capping with QK-norm, and changes attention interleaving to five local layers followed by one global layer starting with a local layer ([Gemma 3 technical report](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/gemma/Gemma3Report.pdf)). Gemma 3’s local attention span is 1024 tokens, its global RoPE base frequency is 1M while local RoPE base frequency is 10K, and its context length is 128K for the larger models and 32K for the 1B model ([Gemma 3 technical report](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/gemma/Gemma3Report.pdf)). Gemma 3 adds image understanding through a frozen SigLIP vision encoder for the 4B/12B/27B variants, with 896×896 image processing and 256 image tokens reported by the model card ([Gemma 3 technical report](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/gemma/Gemma3Report.pdf), [Gemma 3 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_3)). Source strength: **high**.

Official raw Hugging Face `config.json` access for Gemma 2/3 returned authorization errors during this research, so exact Gemma 3 hidden sizes and feed-forward sizes were not treated as primary-source confirmed here unless they came from the technical report or model card ([Gemma 3 technical report](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/gemma/Gemma3Report.pdf), [Gemma 3 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_3)). Source strength: **high** for the access limitation, **unknown** for gated config details.

## 2. Knowledge-editing results on Gemma

The only primary source found here that reports Gemma editing results is AlphaEdit, which states that it extended experiments to “Gemma” and Phi-1.5 and reports Table 3 results for MEMIT, RECT, and AlphaEdit on Gemma across CounterFact and ZsRE ([AlphaEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02355)). AlphaEdit Table 3 reports that, on Gemma/CounterFact, MEMIT scores 64.68 efficacy, 60.36 generalization, 46.73 specificity, 373.94 fluency, and 22.14 consistency; RECT scores 65.17/57.48/52.54/388.77/23.37; and AlphaEdit scores 75.21/67.83/52.63/398.96/32.91 under the same table columns ([AlphaEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02355)). AlphaEdit Table 3 reports that, on Gemma/ZsRE, MEMIT scores 64.38 efficacy, 66.12 generalization, and 24.52 specificity; RECT scores 67.18/64.12/20.02; and AlphaEdit scores 75.91/68.12/23.50 under the same table columns ([AlphaEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02355)). Source strength: **medium-high**, because the paper is primary but the Gemma variant and hyperparameters are not clearly identified in the extracted text.

No primary source found here reports ROME, MEMIT, PMET, EMMET, WISE, GRACE, or MEND experiments on Gemma 4 specifically ([ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229), [PMET](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.08742), [EMMET](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.14236), [WISE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.14768), [GRACE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.11031), [MEND](https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.11309)). No primary source found here reports Gemma results on RippleEdits, MQuAKE, S2RKE, FINE-KED, or any explicit same-entity multi-attribute locality benchmark ([RippleEdits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.12976), [MQuAKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14795), [S2RKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06868), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774)). Source strength: **medium-high**, because absence is based on the primary-source set examined here rather than an exhaustive proof over all possible unpublished code.

EasyEdit is a broad model-editing framework that lists support for methods such as ROME, MEMIT, PMET, EMMET, WISE, GRACE, MEND, AlphaEdit, and UltraEdit, but its README-supported model families emphasize GPT, LLaMA, Baichuan, ChatGLM, InternLM, Qwen, Mistral, and multimodal families rather than Gemma for the classic parameter-editing hparams ([EasyEdit paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.07269), [EasyEdit repository](https://github.com/zjunlp/EasyEdit)). A repository inspection found Gemma-related paths mainly in EasyEdit2 steering and UltraEdit configuration rather than published ROME/MEMIT/PMET/EMMET/WISE/GRACE/MEND Gemma hyperparameters, so Gemma 4 MEMIT would require new hyperparameter search rather than reuse of a mature published config ([EasyEdit repository](https://github.com/zjunlp/EasyEdit)). Source strength: **medium**, because the statement depends on repository state at the time of collection and should be rechecked before implementation.

The standard “locality” metric in ROME/MEMIT-style literature is usually cross-entity or neighborhood preservation, not same-subject preservation of other attributes such as a country’s currency, continent, language, or demonym ([ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229), [RippleEdits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.12976)). Same-entity locality is closer to S2RKE’s same-subject editing problem, DiKE’s “fine-grained irrelevant knowledge” preservation problem, and MEMIT-Merge’s same-subject batch conflict problem than to the classic CounterFact locality metric ([S2RKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06868), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774), [MEMIT-Merge](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.07322)). Source strength: **high** for benchmark definitions, **medium** for mapping those definitions to the proposed country-attribute use case.

## 3. Knowledge localization and causal tracing in Gemma

ROME’s causal tracing result is that factual associations in GPT-style models can be localized to middle-layer feed-forward modules at the subject token, and ROME performs a rank-one MLP weight update to alter a stored factual association ([ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262)). MEMIT generalizes the same locate-and-edit idea to mass editing by modifying a set of critical MLP layers and demonstrates thousands of edits on GPT-J-6B and GPT-NeoX-20B ([MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229)). Source strength: **high** for GPT-family models, **medium** when extrapolated to Gemma.

No primary source found here reports Gemma-specific causal tracing that identifies which Gemma 2/3/4 layers or MLP modules store country-capital/currency/language associations ([Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147), [ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229)). Because Gemma 4 E2B/E4B have PLE residual injections and shared KV behavior, and 12B/26B/31B use 5-local/1-global attention interleaving with very long context, the safest assumption is that GPT-J layer ranges are not transferable without causal tracing and covariance calibration ([Gemma 4 E2B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E2B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Gemma 4 31B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-31B-it/resolve/main/config.json)). Source strength: **high** for architectural differences, **medium-low** for the implementation recommendation.

Gemma 4 introduces several practical complications for MEMIT covariance estimation and causal tracing: final logit soft-capping changes the relationship between hidden-state perturbations and output logits, hybrid local/global attention changes context dependence across layers, the 262K vocabulary increases the importance of tokenizer-level target selection, and PLE in E2B/E4B adds a layer-indexed token identity pathway outside the standard residual stream ([Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py), [Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4)). Gemma 2’s report explicitly documents attention and final logit soft-capping, and Gemma Scope notes that Gemma 2’s logit soft-capping prevents normal centering of the unembedding in TransformerLens workflows ([Gemma 2 technical report](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/gemma/gemma-2-report.pdf), [Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147)). Source strength: **high** for the facts, **medium** for their expected effect on MEMIT.

For dense Gemma 4 variants, the most natural locate-and-edit target remains the MLP output projection because Hugging Face exposes the standard gated MLP `down_proj` and official JAX exposes an equivalent feed-forward output projection named `linear` ([Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py), [google-deepmind/gemma `_modules.py`](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/google-deepmind/gemma/main/gemma/gm/nn/_modules.py)). For 26B A4B, an edit can hit the shared dense `mlp.down_proj`, the per-expert `experts.down_proj`, or both, but no primary source found here provides a Gemma-specific routing-stable covariance recipe for that choice ([Gemma 4 26B A4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-26B-A4B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py), [MoEEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10965)). Source strength: **high** for module structure, **low-medium** for recommended targets.

## 4. Representation disentanglement and polysemanticity

Gemma Scope trained JumpReLU sparse autoencoders on all layers and sublayers of Gemma 2 2B and 9B and selected layers of Gemma 2 27B, with released weights and a Neuronpedia demo for feature inspection ([Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147), [Gemma Scope Hugging Face collection](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-scope), [Gemma Scope Neuronpedia demo](https://neuronpedia.org/gemma-scope)). Gemma Scope trains SAEs at attention outputs, MLP outputs, and post-MLP residual stream locations, which is relevant because MEMIT targets MLP output projections and same-entity interference may occur in the post-MLP residual representation ([Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147)). Source strength: **high**.

Gemma Scope does **not** provide direct evidence that attributes of the same entity are stored in separable subspaces, and the paper itself states that there is no consensus on a reliable metric for SAE or latent-feature quality ([Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147)). Gemma Scope also reports that transcoder reconstruction losses were higher than MLP-output SAE losses and speculates this may relate to Gemma 2’s gated MLP architecture, so a clean MLP-transcoder decomposition for edit control is not guaranteed by the release ([Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147)). Source strength: **high** for the caveat, **medium** for relevance to editing.

DeepMind’s later Gemma Scope 2 technical paper is described as covering all layers and sublayers of Gemma 3 270M, 1B, 4B, 12B, and 27B with updated weights and skip-transcoders, but no primary source found here indicates Gemma Scope coverage for Gemma 4 ([Gemma Scope 2 technical paper](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/DeepMind.com/Blog/gemma-scope-2-helping-the-ai-safety-community-deepen-understanding-of-complex-language-model-behavior/Gemma_Scope_2_Technical_Paper.pdf), [Gemma Scope Hugging Face collection](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-scope)). Source strength: **medium-high**, because Gemma Scope 2 supports Gemma 3 mechanistic work but not a verified Gemma 4 feature dictionary.

DiKE is directly relevant because it frames same-subject preservation as a representation-entanglement problem: existing editors can change target knowledge while failing to preserve fine-grained irrelevant knowledge about the same subject, and DiKE decomposes subject representations into target-related and target-unrelated components before applying a closed-form rank-one update ([DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774)). No primary source found here reports DiKE on Gemma, so Gemma’s suitability for DiKE or null-space/orthogonal-projection editing is unverified ([DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774), [Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147)). Source strength: **high** for DiKE’s problem statement, **unknown** for Gemma applicability.

The current mechanistic evidence does not show that Gemma has less polysemantic or more attribute-disentangled entity representations than GPT-J, Llama, Qwen, or Mistral, and Gemma Scope should be treated as an inspection toolkit rather than proof of same-entity locality ([Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774)). The most defensible hypothesis is that Gemma may enable better diagnostics through released SAEs for Gemma 2/3 lineage, but Gemma 4 same-entity locality still requires direct ablation, causal tracing, and edit-evaluation experiments ([Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147), [Gemma Scope 2 technical paper](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/DeepMind.com/Blog/gemma-scope-2-helping-the-ai-safety-community-deepen-understanding-of-complex-language-model-behavior/Gemma_Scope_2_Technical_Paper.pdf)). Source strength: **medium**.

## 5. Editing mixture-of-experts models

Gemma 4 26B A4B is a sparse MoE with 128 total experts, 8 active experts, and one shared expert according to the model card, and its Hugging Face config sets `enable_moe_block=true`, `num_experts=128`, `top_k_experts=8`, and `moe_intermediate_size=704` ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4), [Gemma 4 26B A4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-26B-A4B-it/resolve/main/config.json)). Hugging Face implements Gemma 4 MoE experts with packed `gate_up_proj` weights and per-expert `down_proj` weights, and the router normalizes hidden states before projecting to expert logits and selecting top-k experts ([Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py)). Source strength: **high**.

MoEEdit frames parameter-modifying knowledge editing for MoE LLMs as a routing-stability problem: dense-model editors can change router inputs and thereby alter expert selection, while MoEEdit uses per-expert null-space projections and block-structured optimization to preserve routing while editing expert parameters ([MoEEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10965)). No primary source found here reports MoEEdit, MEMIT, AlphaEdit, PMET, or EMMET on Gemma 4 26B A4B, and no primary source found here explains how to compute MEMIT covariance across Gemma 4’s 128 experts, top-8 routing, and shared expert ([MoEEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10965), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229), [Gemma 4 26B A4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-26B-A4B-it/resolve/main/config.json)). Source strength: **high** for MoEEdit’s framing, **unknown** for Gemma 4 implementation.

MEMoE and LEMoE are relevant but not MEMIT-class in-weight editing because they add MoE-style adapter or lifelong editing components while keeping original model parameters largely unchanged, so they do not answer whether Gemma 4 26B A4B is editable by a locate-and-edit parameter update ([MEMoE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.19086), [LEMoE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.20030)). For this project, 26B A4B should be treated as a research target only after dense E4B/12B experiments establish a Gemma-specific layer range, subject-token tracing protocol, and same-entity test harness ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4), [MoEEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10965)). Source strength: **medium**.

## 6. Comparative amenability: Gemma vs GPT-J, Llama, Qwen, and Mistral

GPT-J-6B remains the most benchmarked MEMIT substrate because ROME and MEMIT were developed and heavily evaluated on GPT-family causal LMs, including GPT-J-6B for large-scale mass editing ([ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229)). Llama, Qwen, and Mistral have broader modern editing-framework support and appear in more recent editing and same-subject studies than Gemma, including EasyEdit-supported families and same-subject/same-representation work outside Gemma ([EasyEdit repository](https://github.com/zjunlp/EasyEdit), [S2RKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06868), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774), [MEMIT-Merge](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.07322)). Source strength: **high** for framework/paper coverage, **medium** for practical maturity ranking.

Gemma 4’s amenability to dense in-weight editing is mixed: dense variants expose normal gated MLP output projections, tied embeddings, and standard transformer blocks, but E2B/E4B add PLE, all variants use very long-context hybrid attention, and Gemma 4 configs use final logit soft-capping ([Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py), [Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4)). These architectural differences are not disqualifying, but they mean GPT-J/Llama layer ranges, covariance statistics, and tokenization assumptions should not be transplanted without calibration ([MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229), [Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147)). Source strength: **medium-high**.

For same-entity locality, no primary evidence shows Gemma is better than GPT-J, Llama, Qwen, or Mistral, while same-subject editing papers identify subject-representation entanglement and same-subject conflicts as broad failure modes rather than model-specific bugs ([S2RKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06868), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774), [MEMIT-Merge](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.07322)). Mechanistically, a MEMIT-style update keyed by the subject token has no inherent guarantee that “France -> capital” will be orthogonal to “France -> currency,” “France -> continent,” or “France -> language” unless the method explicitly constrains irrelevant same-subject relations or the model’s representation is empirically shown to separate those attributes ([ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774)). Source strength: **medium-high** for the failure-mode literature, **medium** for direct Gemma prediction.

The practical comparative assessment is therefore: GPT-J is best for reproducing classic ROME/MEMIT baselines, Llama/Qwen/Mistral are better-supported modern baselines, Gemma 4 E4B is attractive as a clean Google-lineage dense target with manageable VRAM, and Gemma 4 26B A4B is premature for MEMIT-class same-entity locality because MoE routing adds an unresolved expert-covariance problem ([ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229), [EasyEdit repository](https://github.com/zjunlp/EasyEdit), [MoEEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10965)). Source strength: **medium**.

## 7. Practical recommendation for a single 24 GB GPU

The best single-24 GB GPU target is Gemma 4 E4B dense, because the official model card reports BF16 weights with 20% overhead at 17.9 GB for E4B, while 12B is 26.7 GB, 26B A4B is 57.7 GB, and 31B is 69.9 GB under the same accounting ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4)). E2B is the safest memory target at 11.4 GB BF16 but gives less capacity, while E4B provides the strongest dense candidate that still leaves some memory for activations, covariance batches, and edit evaluation on a 24 GB card ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4), [Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json)). Source strength: **high** for memory estimates, **medium** for best-target recommendation.

Quantized Q4 weights are much smaller in the model card table, but MEMIT-class parameter editing normally requires reliable full-precision or at least dequantized weight updates and covariance calculations, so Q4 should be treated as an inference/deployment path rather than the primary editing path unless a quantized-editing experiment validates it ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229)). Source strength: **medium**.

Hugging Face Gemma 4 support exists in the Transformers Gemma 4 and Gemma 4 Unified implementation files, and the Gemma 4 configs identify `Gemma4ForConditionalGeneration` or `Gemma4UnifiedForConditionalGeneration` architectures ([Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py), [Hugging Face Gemma 4 Unified implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4_unified/modeling_gemma4_unified.py), [Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json)). Because the configs reference development-era Transformers versions, an implementation should pin a Transformers commit known to include Gemma 4 support and verify module names before applying an editor ([Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py)). Source strength: **high** for support files, **medium** for pinning advice.

Tokenizer single-token behavior for short factual targets such as capitals, currencies, and languages remains unverified in this research because tokenizer files were not loaded and tested directly ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4)). A primary experiment should run `AutoTokenizer.from_pretrained("google/gemma-4-E4B-it")` and record token IDs for each target answer under leading-space, no-leading-space, and sentence-context forms before selecting a MEMIT object-token objective ([Gemma 2 technical report](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/gemma/gemma-2-report.pdf), [Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json)). Source strength: **low-medium**, because it is a practical requirement rather than a published result.

Recommended first experiment: run causal tracing on Gemma 4 E4B for a curated country-attribute set, estimate covariance for candidate middle MLP `down_proj` layers, apply single-attribute edits to one relation, and evaluate same-subject holdouts for currency, continent, official language, demonym, population bucket, and neighboring countries alongside classic CounterFact-style neighborhood specificity ([ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229), [S2RKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06868), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774)). The initial pass should compare unmodified MEMIT, AlphaEdit-style null-space projection, and a DiKE-style subject-representation decomposition if Gemma hidden-state features permit stable decomposition ([AlphaEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02355), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774), [Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147)). Source strength: **medium**.

## Unknowns and required primary experiments

1. **Gemma 4 same-entity locality is unmeasured.** No primary source found here evaluates Gemma 4 on same-subject or same-entity locality benchmarks such as S2RKE, FINE-KED, RippleEdits, or MQuAKE ([S2RKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06868), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774), [RippleEdits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.12976), [MQuAKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.14795)).
2. **Published Gemma editing hyperparameters are not established.** AlphaEdit reports an unspecified Gemma result, but no primary source found here provides Gemma 4 MEMIT layer ranges, covariance datasets, rewrite templates, or preservation-layer choices ([AlphaEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02355), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229)).
3. **Gemma-specific factual localization is unknown.** No primary source found here identifies the Gemma 4 layers, token positions, or modules where country-capital, currency, language, and continent associations live ([ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262), [Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147)).
4. **PLE’s effect on editing is unknown.** E2B/E4B PLE mechanics are clear in the implementation, but no primary source found here measures whether PLE increases entity-attribute disentanglement or creates additional edit side effects ([Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py)).
5. **Gemma Scope does not prove attribute disentanglement.** Gemma Scope enables feature-level inspection for Gemma 2 and Gemma Scope 2 covers Gemma 3, but no primary source found here shows same-entity attribute subspace separability or Gemma 4 SAE coverage ([Gemma Scope](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.05147), [Gemma Scope 2 technical paper](https://storage.googleapis.com/deepmind-media/DeepMind.com/Blog/gemma-scope-2-helping-the-ai-safety-community-deepen-understanding-of-complex-language-model-behavior/Gemma_Scope_2_Technical_Paper.pdf)).
6. **MoE editing for Gemma 4 26B A4B is unresolved.** MoEEdit provides a general routing-stable direction for MoE parameter editing, but no primary source found here applies it to Gemma 4’s 128-expert, top-8 architecture ([MoEEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.10965), [Gemma 4 26B A4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-26B-A4B-it/resolve/main/config.json)).
7. **Tokenizer object-token behavior must be measured.** The 262K vocabulary is documented, but the single-token status of specific capitals, currencies, and languages under Gemma 4’s tokenizer was not verified here ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4)).
8. **Instruction-tuned vs base behavior is unverified.** The configs examined are instruction-tuned model configs, and a same-entity editing engine should test whether base and instruction-tuned checkpoints differ in factual localization, refusal/safety interference, and edit generalization ([Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4)).

## Final one-paragraph assessment

Gemma 4 E4B is the most promising practical Gemma target for a first in-weight same-entity locality experiment because it is dense, fits 24 GB BF16 better than 12B/26B/31B, and exposes standard gated-MLP output projections that MEMIT-class methods can target ([Gemma 4 model card](https://ai.google.dev/gemma/docs/core/model_card_4), [Gemma 4 E4B config](https://huggingface.co/google/gemma-4-E4B-it/resolve/main/config.json), [Hugging Face Gemma 4 implementation](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/huggingface/transformers/main/src/transformers/models/gemma4/modeling_gemma4.py)). However, the current evidence predicts Gemma 4 inherits the model-general same-subject non-locality failure unless the editor explicitly separates target-related and target-unrelated subject components, because the most relevant same-subject literature identifies entangled subject representations and key-value conflicts as the core problem rather than a quirk of GPT-J alone ([S2RKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06868), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774), [MEMIT-Merge](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.07322)). The decisive next step is not another architecture review but a primary Gemma 4 E4B experiment that measures country-level same-entity holdouts before and after MEMIT, AlphaEdit/null-space, and DiKE-style updates ([ROME](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.05262), [MEMIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.07229), [AlphaEdit](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02355), [DiKE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.18774)).
