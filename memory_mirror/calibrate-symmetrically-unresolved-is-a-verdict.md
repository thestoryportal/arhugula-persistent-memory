In B1 (size term) I over-claimed in BOTH directions on the same data, ~30 min apart:
1. **Over-claim A:** "SIZE-PROTECTS" from early seeds 1–2 (advisor: don't over-read early seeds — n=3 underpowered).
2. **Over-correction B:** after a later seed reversed it and a re-run proved that reversal was a noise draw, I swung to "size-protection is **refuted / null**." Advisor caught it: strip the proven-noise draw and 7B leans *less*-corrupted in **7/8 paired cells (+11.5pp)** — the protective lean is the better-supported direction; you can't confirm it (≪ ~50pp run-noise) but you **can't refute it either**. The collapse was the artifact, not the protection.

**Why:** "I over-claimed X" tempts a correction to "not-X." But the correct correction is almost always to **UNRESOLVED**, not to the opposite pole. A noisy mean (here −2.3pp) can read as a null while the **sign pattern** (7/8 cells one way, after removing the noise outlier) says otherwise — the [[deterministic-null-label-can-hide-real-effect]] trap, mirrored.

**How to apply:**
- When you catch an over-claim, ask "does the data support the *opposite*, or just *not my claim*?" Default the correction to **UNRESOLVED / weak-lean-direction-X**, not to the negation.
- Apply [[pass-label-not-equal-promotable-claim]] **symmetrically** — a mechanical NULL/refutation label is as much "not a promotable claim" as a mechanical PASS.
- "Unresolved, weak lean, instrument-noise-limited" is a **real, honest verdict** — often stronger and truer than either "confirmed" or "null." State that the verdict *flips with one noise draw* as the finding.
- Read the **paired sign pattern** (cells favoring each side, after removing proven-noise draws), not just the noise-dominated mean. [[match-metric-to-the-claim]]
