I twice concluded a path was "blocked / needs LARQL development." Both times the operator pushed — "are you ABSOLUTELY certain nothing's overlooked, including no-code or creative solutions?" — and the push surfaced REAL overlooked, no-code wins: **ROUTE VERIFY** (fixed the cross-entity bleed I'd called unfixable), **rollback-by-serving-base** (governance I'd called failed), **COMPILE** (bakes overlays→weights), and the **`.vlp`-injection bridge** (clean+in-weight+governed via existing LARQL commands + a small Python generator — no LARQL code). I had stopped searching too early and declared a blocker.

**Why:** Premature "blocked" conclusions are expensive and often wrong — there are usually config/LQL-level, creative, or compose-existing-primitives options left unexplored. The operator's certainty-challenges are a reliable signal that I've under-searched, not nagging.

**How to apply:** Before declaring "blocked / requires building X," exhaustively enumerate options: config/flags, existing commands/primitives composed differently, reading the tool's source for capabilities, no-code/creative routes — and verify, don't assert. Treat the operator's "are you sure / what are we missing" as a prompt to genuinely re-search, not to re-defend. See also [[operator-profile-llm-as-database]].
