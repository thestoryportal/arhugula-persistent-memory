Distinguish two different things a single seed does and doesn't establish:
- **Significance / "is the effect real here":** can be solid at ONE seed, because the held-out items are independent observations. D1 Phase 2 seed0: concentrated 5/12 vs diluted 12/12 → Fisher two-sided p≈0.005. The effect is statistically real at that seed.
- **Generality / "does it hold beyond this sample":** is what single-seed CANNOT establish — entity sample, write-ordering, model-specific idiosyncrasy. That's what replication (≥3 seeds, ≥2 orderings) buys.

**Why (2026-06-20, advisor):** I had conflated "single-seed" with "underpowered/shaky" and was *underselling* a real D1 effect by hedging on the single-seed p-value. The advisor: "single-seed limits generality, not reality — state the split explicitly." Then (Phase 3) the OPPOSITE risk appeared and the cross-family reviewer warned not to *over*-lean on a single-seed/low-power p as a headline effect size.

**How to apply:** report the within-seed significance AND the replication separately; let the replication carry the *generality* claim and the within-seed test carry the *existence* claim. Don't collapse them. Pairs with [[deterministic-null-label-can-hide-real-effect]] and [[pass-label-not-equal-promotable-claim]].
